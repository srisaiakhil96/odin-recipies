Dosa Recipe

A dosa, also called dosai, is a thin pancake in South Indian cuisine made from a fermented batter of ground black lentils and rice. Dosas are popular in South Asia as well as around the world. Dosas are served hot, often with chutney and sambar.

The ingrediants required are as follows

  • Urad dal (Split black lentils): 1 cup
  • Parboiled rice or idli rice: 2 to 3 cups
  • Fenugreek seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: as per taste
  • Water: for soaking and grinding
  • Cooking oil: for making dosas

You can prepare a delicious dosa by following the below steps

  1. Soaking: The urad dal and rice are soaked separately for around 4-6 hours. Fenugreek seeds can be soaked along with urad dal.
  2. Grinding: After soaking, they are ground to a fine paste separately, then mixed together. The mixture should be of pouring consistency.
  3. Fermentation: Salt is added to the mixture, which is then left to ferment overnight (or for 8-10 hours). It will rise and double in volume.
  4. Making dosa: Heat a dosa pan or griddle, pour a ladle of batter in the center and spread it in a circular motion. Drizzle some oil around the edges and cook until the dosa is golden brown. Flip and cook the other side if desired.
  5. Serving: Dosa is usually served hot, often with sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew) and coconut chutney.
