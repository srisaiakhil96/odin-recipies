Idly Recipe

Idli is another popular South Indian dish. It's a type of steamed rice cake that's made from a fermented batter, similar to dosa.

Here are the main ingredients typically required to make idli:

  • Urad dal (Split black lentils): 1 cup
  • Idli rice or parboiled rice: 4 cups
  • Fenugreek seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: as per taste
  • Water: for soaking and grinding

Here's a basic process of making idli:

  1. Soaking: The urad dal and rice are soaked separately for around 4-6 hours. Fenugreek seeds can be soaked along with urad dal.
  2. Grinding: After soaking, they are ground to a fine paste separately, then mixed together. The mixture should be of a pouring consistency, but slightly thicker than dosa batter.
  3. Fermentation: Salt is added to the mixture, which is then left to ferment overnight (or for 8-10 hours). The batter will rise and double in volume.
  4. Making idli: After fermentation, the batter is poured into idli molds and steamed for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Serving: Idli is usually served hot with sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew) and coconut chutney.
